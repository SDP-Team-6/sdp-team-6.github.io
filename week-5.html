Week 5 - QA Stuff

The main focus of the team this week was on improving the Webots Simulation and working towards getting a working version of the Hardware prototype.

The hardware team coordinated with the technicians to build a LEGO casing for PAUL and attach the motors into the case. They took the decision to use a Raspberry Pi over an Arduino as the technicians advised this would allow us to work remotely more easily. They also followed the tutorial and managed to connect to the Raspberry Pi called Pikachu and run the motors successfully.

The software team met with the Webots Expert to discuss the best practices for simulating friction in webots. Yasmin conducted research into UVC cleaning which gave us a great deal of knowledge of how to best design our UV module. Stirling and Gagan attended the Quantitative Analysis workshop on behalf of the team and will use what they learned in the next demo report.

We had our weekly meeting on Thursday, where we discussed what each team had been working on, heard about the QA workshop and looked at the UVC research by Yasmin. We plan to keep working during flexible learning week and the technicians also said they would be working.
