Team Organisation

To allow group members to focus on the areas of the project they are most interested in and more professional at, we decided to use a functional organizational structure. We created sub-teams which will work on specific tasks where each sub-team has a team leader who supervises the team to achieve goals in the project plan.

Sub-team Responsibilities Team Leader Team Members
Project Management Responsible for the effective management of the groups’ resources and keeping track of deadlines. Will also be responsible for drafting reports and collecting feedback from clients. Daniel Stirling, Gagan
Web Development Responsible for the development of the web interface used to control the robot remotely and handling large-scale deployment of our robot. Gagan Jim, Zhiqi, Naman
Software and Simulation Responsible for the development of the software which will control the robot and producing a Webots (or similar software) simulation of the robot. Stirling Naman, Yasmin, Jim, Zhiqi, Gagan
Hardware Responsible for the design of the hardware components, liaising with technicians and integration of the software into the hardware Pablo Daniel


Agile Development

As this course required us to demonstrate every two weeks, we decided to embrace an agile methology in our team. Throughout the course, we welcomed changing requirements from our clients, even late in development, and gave continious attention to technical excellence and good design which in turn enhanced agility.

Productivity Tools

To aid our group collaboration we use a range of productivity tools:

MS Teams & Communications

  • Teams allowed our team to collaborate remotely. Our team worked from three different countries, teams allowed us to host meetings, chat, and even pair-program for long duration.
  • We made one channel for each team to work on, one for the whole team called "General", and one for memes. Sadly the team was too productive & no memes were shared.
  • Every week, every team attended three meeting: one with everyone on monday, one sub-team meeting on tuesday, and one weekly team meeting We have a Weekly meeting with all team members on Teams in order to see at what stage we are at and discuss the work planned for each member for the week ahead

    • Trello & SCRUM

  • This platform provides a flexible and transparent board which will allow us to track what individual members are working on.
  • It also allows us to track deadlines and provide an overview of upcoming events.
  • It is nicely integrated into Teams which our communication and task allocation are all within one application.

    • Git & GitHub

  • We will use repositories to split code up into individual areas (such as the Webots simulation, Hardware ROS code and the Website code).
  • Sub-teams will be responsible for their respective repositories but should strive to only submit high-quality code.
  • To ensure code quality each Github commit should be peer-reviewed by another member of the group. If the code does not meet the sub-team’s coding standards then the code should be sent back to the author with constructive comments on how to rectify their code

    • Overleaf & Latex

  • It also allows us to track deadlines and provide an overview of upcoming events.
